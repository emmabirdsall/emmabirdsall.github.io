Who is Molly Malone?

Molly Malone is a fictional character. Her tale comes from a song in which she is described as a fishwife selling her trade on the streets of Dublin and she dies young from a fever. The below song has been recorded by many famous artists, including Bing Crosby and Rosemary Clooney, Pete Seeger, Sinéad O'Connor, Burl Ives, Danny Kaye and The Dubliners. You can read more about the song and legend on Wikipedia.

Our Love of Ireland

We opened Molly Malone's after a trip to Ireland in the winter of 2019. We immediately fell in love with the beauty of the landscape and the incredibly welcoming people. We spent our first night in Dublin, then drove to the Cliffs of Moher and continued on to Killarney where we made our home base. We went on a Jaunting Cars tour. (Spoiler alert: You are pulled in a cart by a horse, there are no cars involved)

We also went on a guided walking tour of Killarney National Park and drove the Ring of Kerry. One day we climbed to the top of Dunsmore Head, which is the western-most location in Europe. And to round out the trip we explored Galway and spent some more time in Dublin.

I would absolutely recommend a trip to Ireland. The scenery is breathtaking and the people are so welcoming. There's history everywhere and you'll have a trip of a lifetime. We saw so many amazing things, but our favorite part was exploring the towns by going into their pubs. Each pub was different, but they all had a cozy and inviting atmosphere that made us feel at home.

We hope you get the same feeling being at Molly Malone's. It's our way of bringing some of Ireland's magic to you.
